🌍✨ Evolution of Landforms due to Internal Forces

(The Invisible Architects of the Earth)

Imagine the Earth as a living planet with a burning heart inside.
That inner energy constantly reshapes the surface — slowly, silently, and sometimes violently.

Let us understand this beautifully and clearly.

🔥 1️⃣ Source of Energy – The Power Behind the Movements

🌡️ A. Internal Heat – The Earth’s Hidden Fire

  • The Earth’s core is extremely hot.
  • This heat comes from:
  • 🔹 Residual heat from planetary formation
  • 🔹 Radioactive decay of uranium, thorium & potassium

👉 This heat creates pressure.
👉 Pressure creates movement.
👉 Movement reshapes the crust.

Without internal heat, there would be no mountains, no volcanoes, no earthquakes.

🧪 B. Chemical Reactions – Silent Rock Transformation

Inside the crust:

  • High temperature + high pressure = Metamorphism

Rocks change form without melting.

Example:

  • Limestone → Marble
  • Shale → Slate

It is like baking clay into pottery — shape changes due to internal conditions.

🌋 C. Endogenic Forces – The Structure Builders

  • These forces originate inside Earth.
  • They create relief (height differences).
  • But shaping is completed later by:
  • Rivers
  • Wind
  • Glaciers

👉 Internal forces = Builders
👉 External forces = Sculptors

🌏 2️⃣ Earth Movements (Tectonic Movements)

Also called:

  • Plate movements
  • Tectonic movements

These movements:

  • Change the Earth’s crust.
  • Influence mountains, plateaus, valleys.
  • Cause earthquakes and volcanoes.

They are classified into:

1️⃣ Slow Movements
2️⃣ Sudden Movements

🐢 I. Slow Movements – The Patient Engineers

These movements take thousands to millions of years.

They are of two types:

  • Vertical Movements
  • Horizontal Movements

⬆️ A. Vertical Movements

1️⃣ Uplift (Epeirogenic Movement)

Also called:

  • Continent builders
  • Plateau builders

Meaning:

  • Large landmass slowly rises.

Features:

  • Very slow.
  • Wide area.
  • Rock layers remain mostly undisturbed.

Result:

  • Former sea beds become land.

🌄 Example: The Himalayas

  • Himalayas
  • Formed due to compression between Indian and Eurasian plates.
  • Tethys Sea sediments were uplifted.
  • Still rising today!

💡 Proof that Earth is still active.

2️⃣ Subsidence

Meaning:

  • Land slowly sinks relative to surrounding area.

Examples:

  • Dwarka (India)
  • Submerged forests of Tirunelveli
  • Bombay harbour
  • Rann of Kutch

This happens due to:

  • Tectonic weakness
  • Crustal adjustments

It is like a mattress sinking where weight is applied.

↔️ B. Horizontal Movements – The Powerful Shapers

Two types:

1️⃣ Compression
2️⃣ Tension

🏔️ 1. Compression (Orogenic Movement)

Meaning:

  • Plates move towards each other.
  • Rocks bend.
  • Folds form.

Result:

  • Fold Mountains

Examples

  • Alps
  • Andes
  • Rocky Mountains
  • Appalachians

Sedimentary rocks are more vulnerable — they fold easily.

Compression = Mountain builder.

🌊 2. Tension

Meaning:

  • Plates move away from each other.
  • Cracks develop.
  • Faults are formed.

Results:

  • Fault block mountains
  • Rift valleys

🌍 Example: Rhine Rift Valley

  • Black Forest
  • Vosges Mountains
  • Narmada & Tapi rift valleys (India)

Escarpments (steep slopes) are common.

Important:
Rift valleys are early stages of ocean formation.

⚡ II. Sudden Movements – The Violent Episodes

These happen quickly and can cause disasters.

Two types:

1️⃣ Earthquakes
2️⃣ Volcanic eruptions

🌎 1️⃣ Earthquake – The Shaking Earth

Meaning:

  • Sudden tremor of Earth’s surface.

Causes:

  • Folding
  • Faulting
  • Rock displacement
  • Volcanic activity

Important Terms

  • Focus → Origin point inside Earth
  • Epicentre → Point above focus on surface
  • Seismograph → Measures earthquake

Intensity decreases as we move away from epicentre.

🌋 Distribution

More frequent in:

  • Circum-Pacific belt
  • Mid-Asian mountain belt

Damage depends more on:

  • Population density
  • Building quality

🌋 2️⃣ Volcanic Eruption – The Fire Fountain

Meaning:

  • Magma rises due to pressure.
  • Erupts through weak points.
  • Forms cone-shaped volcano with crater.

Process: Volcanism

Types:

  • Active
  • Dormant
  • Extinct

Classification can change if activity resumes.

🌋 Lava Plateau Formation

  • Deccan Traps
  • Malwa Plateau
  • Columbia Plateau (USA)
  • Paraná Plateau (South America)

Lava spreads and solidifies — forming vast plateaus.

🌟 Overall Impact of Internal Forces

Slow Movements Create:

  • Mountains
  • Plateaus
  • Rift valleys
  • Raised & submerged coasts

Sudden Movements Cause:

  • Earthquakes
  • Tsunamis
  • Volcanoes
  • Landslides
  • New land formation

👉 Internal forces are the architects of Earth’s structure.
👉 External forces are the artists that refine it.

The Earth is not static.
It is alive.
It is evolving.
And these invisible internal forces are shaping our planet every second. 🌍🔥
